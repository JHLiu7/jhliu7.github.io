Jinghui Liu

Research Scientist, Australian e-Health Research Centre, CSIRO.

Brisbane, QLD, Australia

Email: jinghui.liu(at)csiro.au

I am a Research Scientist at the Australian e-Health Research Centre, CSIRO. My research focuses on applying natural language processing and language models in healthcare to improve care quality, increase healthcare system efficiency, and promote population health. I’m interested in various applications analysing clinical data from electronic health records, including clinical documentation generation, assisted medical coding, and predictive analysis for decision support. Before my current role, I was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at CSIRO (2023–2026), where I worked on LLM-driven AI for health as part of CSIRO’s initiatives on clinical AI and minimising antimicrobial resistance. I completed my PhD at the University of Melbourne in 2023, supervised by Prof. Karin Verspoor, Prof. Daniel Capurro, and Dr Anthony Nguyen. I also hold a Master of Health Informatics from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

research interests

  • Clinical documentation improvement — clinical documentation generation and summarisation using LLMs.
  • Generative information extraction for health — extracting and structuring information from clinical reports.
  • Synthetic clinical text — supporting privacy-preserving model development in medical scenarios.
  • Predictive analytics on EHRs — multimodal modelling and predictive analysis using clinical records.
  • Medical coding — version-independent models for ICD and related terminologies.
